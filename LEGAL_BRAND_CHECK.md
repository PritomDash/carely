# Carely — Brand & Copyright Risk Check

Researched 2026-07-07. This is an informational risk assessment, not legal advice — for a real launch, a Bangladesh-qualified trademark attorney should do a formal clearance search before you commit budget to marketing under this name.

## 1. Logo

**Finding: safe to use.**

The pulse-heart logo (`carely-frontend/src/components/CarelyLogo.js`) is original SVG artwork — a custom heart-shaped `<path>` combined with a custom `<polyline>` ECG/pulse line, built from scratch for this project rather than traced or copied from an existing icon set or brand. A heart shape combined with a heartbeat/pulse line is a very common visual motif across the healthcare industry generally (used by everything from hospitals to fitness apps to blood-donation charities) — the *concept* isn't ownable, only a specific expression of it is, and this expression is original. No further action needed here.

## 2. The name "Carely"

**Finding: real risk, not low. Worth a considered decision, not a rubber stamp.**

There are multiple existing products actively using "Carely" in the same general space:

- **Carely, Inc. (US)** — operates "Carely Family" and "Carely Community," caregiving *communication/coordination* apps for families and professional care teams, used by 30,000+ families. Search results show a "Carely®" registered trademark. This is a different business model from a booking marketplace (they're a communication tool, not a "find and book a professional" platform), which reduces confusion risk somewhat — but the trademark class ("providing a website featuring information in the field of caregiving," roughly) may still be read broadly enough to cover adjacent care-coordination services.
- **"Carely – Caregiver Listings"** (Google Play, package `co.carely.android`) — this is the closest match by business model: an app to find babysitters, nurses for home care, and elderly caregivers, and browse caregiver profiles. This is *the same product category* as this app (a caregiver marketplace/listings platform), just under a different name than the Inc. above, suggesting the name is already used more than once for exactly this kind of product.
- **"Carely Health"**, **"Carely: Lifestyle Health Check"**, **"Carely" (medication reminder)** — several more apps in adjacent health/wellness niches, unrelated business models but same name, on the same app stores.

**What this means practically:**
- On Google Play / Apple App Store, multiple apps already coexist under "Carely" (evidently the stores don't block exact-name reuse), so *listing rejection* is unlikely.
- The *legal* risk is trademark confusion in a shared or overlapping market. Trademark rights are territorial — a US registration doesn't automatically block use in Bangladesh — but if this app ever expands beyond Bangladesh, or if any of these existing "Carely" products expand into Bangladesh or claim international trademark rights, a conflict becomes more plausible given how close "Carely – Caregiver Listings" is in concept.
- I could not find a specific "Carely" trademark record in Bangladesh's DPDT (Department of Patents, Designs and Trademarks) database via general web search — DPDT's own search isn't well-indexed by search engines, so **absence of a hit here is not proof of absence**; it would need a direct search at dpdt.gov.bd or a search through a Bangladeshi trademark agent.

**Risk assessment: Medium.** Not "obviously infringing" (different registrants, no evidence of an active Bangladesh registration), but not "clearly safe" either, given how many products already use this exact name for this exact kind of business.

**Recommendations (pick one, this is your call):**
1. **Do a real DPDT search before any paid marketing push.** This is cheap and fast (a Bangladeshi IP agent can typically do a knockout search for a small fee) and would settle the "is anyone already registered in-market" question definitively.
2. **Register "Carely" (or your final name) with DPDT yourself** once you've cleared it — first-to-file matters, and this locks in your own protection in Bangladesh rather than leaving the name open for someone else to claim.
3. **If you want zero risk**, a name tweak (e.g. "CarelyBD", "Carely Bangladesh", or a fully distinct name) sidesteps the overlap with the existing apps above while keeping the logo and most branding intact.

I have **not** changed the name or logo — this file is for your decision.
